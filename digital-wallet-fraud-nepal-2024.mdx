## The Rise of Digital Wallet Crime

With over 15 million eSewa users and millions more on Khalti and IME Pay, digital wallets have become prime targets for cybercriminals in Nepal. In 2023-2024, fraud cases involving these platforms increased by an estimated 300% compared to the previous year.

## Common Attack Patterns

### 1. Fake Customer Support Calls
The most prevalent method. Attackers call victims claiming to be wallet support, citing "account suspension" or "KYC updates." They then:
- Guide victims to share OTPs
- Request screen sharing via AnyDesk/TeamViewer
- Initiate unauthorized transfers

### 2. QR Code Manipulation
Attackers at busy locations (like Ratnapark) replace merchant QR codes with their own. Victims think they're paying a vendor but are actually sending money to the attacker.

### 3. Loan App Exploitation
Predatory loan apps request excessive permissions, then use harvested data to access linked digital wallets.

### 4. SIM Swap + Wallet Drain
As covered in our SIM swap article, once attackers control your number, they reset your wallet PIN and drain the balance.

## Case Study: The Fake Investment Scheme

In February 2024, Cyber Bureau arrested a group running fake "crypto investment" schemes through Telegram. They collected initial deposits via eSewa, showed fake "profits" in their app, then disappeared with NPR 2.5 crore from 300+ victims.

## Platform Responses

### eSewa
- Introduced transaction delay for new payees
- Added voice-based OTP for high-value transactions
- 24/7 fraud monitoring team

### Khalti
- Implemented device binding
- Added biometric authentication option
- In-app suspicious activity alerts

## Prevention Checklist

| Action | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Never share OTPs—even with "support" | ⚠️ Critical |
| Enable app-level biometric lock | Recommended |
| Verify QR codes belong to merchant | Before paying |
| Use unique passwords | Essential |
| Monitor transactions daily | Best practice |

## What Nepal Needs

1. **Unified Fraud Database**: Share threat intel between wallet providers.
2. **Real-time Transaction Blocking**: Allow users to freeze accounts via SMS.
3. **Liability Framework**: Clear rules on who pays when fraud occurs.
4. **Financial Literacy Campaigns**: Target rural areas with basic security awareness.
