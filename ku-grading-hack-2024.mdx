## The Incident
In late 2024, Kathmandu University's (KU) digital grading portal was compromised. Unlike typical external hacks, forensic analysis revealed that the breach utilized compromised faculty credentials. 

The attackers gained administrative access to the central academic database and covertly altered the final grades of several dozen students just days before the official graduation lists were published. 

## The Impact
The manipulation of grades struck at the very core of the university's academic integrity. The discovery forced an immediate halt to the graduation processing, triggering a massive, manual audit of thousands of physical exam papers against the digital records to identify and revert the fraudulent changes.

## Lessons Learned
1. **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**: Administrative access to critical databases must require strict MFA. A stolen password should never be enough to alter official academic records.
2. **Immutable Audit Logs**: The database must employ append-only, immutable logging (potentially utilizing DLT/Blockchain concepts) so that any change to a grade is cryptographically recorded, timestamped, and cannot be deleted by an attacker to cover their tracks.
3. **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**: Faculty should only have write-access to their specific courses during a narrow grading window, after which the records should be locked and require high-level authorization to amend.
