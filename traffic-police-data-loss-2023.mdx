## The Incident
May 2023. The **Kathmandu Valley Traffic Police** dropped a bombshell: their Integrated Traffic Violation Record System (TVRS) had crashed. The data? Gone. "Irretrievable."

Years of traffic fines, license suspension records, and payment histories vanished into the digital void. Drivers who had paid fines had no proof. Pending fines disappeared. It was chaos. And it wasn't hackers—it was us.

## What Went Wrong?
This wasn't an APT or a ransomware gang. It was a failure of basics.

### The Root Causes
1.  **The "Live" Migration**: The contractor tried to migrate the live production database to a new server. Rule #1 of IT: You never touch Production until Staging is verifying 100%. They broke Rule #1.
2.  **Schrödinger's Backup**: When the primary server corrupted, they went to the backups. The backups were either corrupted, too old, or allegedly stored on the *same physical disk* as the live data.
3.  **No Safety Net**: There was no "Hot Standby" or Disaster Recovery (DR) site. When Server A died, there was no Server B to take the load.

## The Impact
*   **Back to Pen and Paper**: Traffic officers in 2023 were forced to write tickets by hand. 
*   **Revenue Black Hole**: The government lost untold millions in uncollectable fines.
*   **Trust Deficit**: How can a citizen trust a digital government ID if the government can't even keep the file open?

## The Takeaway
1.  **The 3-2-1 Rule**: It's not optional. **3** copies of data, **2** different media types, **1** copy offsite (cloud/tape). If you don't have this, you don't have data.
2.  **Test Your Restores**: A backup isn't real until you've restored from it. If you haven't tested your restore process in 6 months, you have no backups.
3.  **Staging Environments**: Never, ever migrate a live government database without a full dry run in a sandboxed staging environment.
