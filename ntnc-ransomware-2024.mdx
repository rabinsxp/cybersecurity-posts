## The Incident
In September 2024, the National Trust for Nature Conservation (NTNC) suffered a debilitating ransomware attack. Cybercriminals breached the organization's legacy internal networks through a phishing email that delivered a sophisticated ransomware payload. 

The malware rapidly encrypted decades of invaluable wildlife tracking data, conservation research, and financial records. The attackers demanded a significant ransom in cryptocurrency for the decryption keys.

## The Impact
The attack brought the NTNC's daily operations to a standstill. Researchers were locked out of critical longitudinal data tracking endangered species (like the Bengal Tiger and One-Horned Rhino). The organization had to revert to pen-and-paper for weeks, severely disrupting international conservation reporting and funding proposals.

## Lessons Learned
1. **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)**: Traditional antivirus software failed to detect the polymorphic ransomware. EDR solutions are required to monitor process behavior and kill malicious encryption attempts instantly.
2. **Offline Backups**: The NTNC's network-attached backups were also encrypted. Organizations must maintain immutable, offline backups (air-gapped) that ransomware cannot traverse to.
3. **Phishing Awareness Training**: The entire breach stemmed from a single compromised workstation. Regular, simulated phishing campaigns are essential to harden the "human firewall."
