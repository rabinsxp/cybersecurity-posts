## Overview

In November 2023, the Government of Nepal approved the updated National Cyber Security Policy, replacing the draft framework that had been in limbo since 2016. This marks a significant step toward formalizing Nepal's cyber defense posture.

## Key Provisions

### 1. National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC)
The policy establishes the NCSC under the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology (MoCIT). The NCSC will serve as the national Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT).

### 2. Critical Information Infrastructure Protection (CIIP)
For the first time, Nepal has formally categorized critical infrastructure sectors:
- Banking and Finance
- Energy and Power
- Telecommunications
- Government Services
- Healthcare

Operators in these sectors must implement baseline security controls and report incidents.

### 3. Mandatory Security Audits
Government agencies and critical infrastructure operators must conduct annual security audits by accredited firms.

### 4. Cyber Security Education
The policy mandates integration of cyber security into the national curriculum and promotes certification programs.

## What's Missing

### No Data Protection Law
The policy focuses on national security but does not address personal data protection. Citizens still have no legal recourse for data breaches.

### Vague Enforcement
While requirements are outlined, penalties for non-compliance remain undefined.

### Limited Private Sector Scope
Non-critical private businesses are largely unaffected, despite being frequent breach victims.

## Implementation Timeline

| Milestone | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|
| NCSC Operational | Q2 2024 |
| CIIP Guidelines Published | Q3 2024 |
| First Compliance Audits | Q1 2025 |

## Expert Opinion

The policy is a necessary foundation but not sufficient. Without an accompanying Data Protection Act and clear enforcement mechanisms, it risks becoming another well-intentioned document gathering dust.

## What Organizations Should Do Now

1. **Identify if you're CIIP**: Review sector classifications to determine obligations.
2. **Baseline Assessment**: Conduct internal security assessments now.
3. **Vendor Vetting**: Prepare for audits by identifying accredited security firms.
4. **Train Staff**: Awareness training is explicitly mentioned as a requirement.
