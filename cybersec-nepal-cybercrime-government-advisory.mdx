# Cyber Security in Nepal: The Digital Battlefield

Nepal is in the middle of a digital growth spurt, but our defenses haven't kept up. Recent months have been a wake-up call—from crippling government server crashes to policy pivots on TikTok. The message is clear: the "security by obscurity" era is over.

## The Current Landscape

It's getting noisy out there. **The Kathmandu Post** recently reported that cybercrime cases nearly doubled in a single year—jumping from **9,013** to **19,730**. That's not just a statistic; that's a warning light. It means our businesses, our government, and our citizens are being targeted faster than we can patch our systems.

In response, the government is finally moving. We saw the issuance of a **102-Point Advisory** for government IT security—basically a "Do's and Don'ts" list for protecting national data. We also saw the **TikTok Ban** get lifted, but only after ByteDance agreed to play ball with Nepalese law enforcement. It shows a shift from "ban everything" to "regulate and collaborate."

## The Weak Points?
Despite the advisories, the cracks are showing.
*   **Vendor Lock-in**: Too many government systems are built by contractors who hand over the keys but not the source code. When things break, the government is helpless.
*   **The "Paper Tiger" Policy**: We have guidelines, but no dedicated **Cybersecurity Act** with teeth. Advisories are suggestions; laws are mandates. Without audits and penalties, security is just a nice-to-have.

## The Path Forward
We don't need more awareness seminars; we need engineering and enforcement.
1.  **Stop Blaming Users**: Yes, awareness helps. But better systems prevent users from making mistakes. Force 2FA. default.
2.  **Sovereign Tech**: We need to own our stack. The government needs an in-house "Cyber Force" of engineers, not just policy writers.
3.  **Global Intel**: Cybercrime is borderless. Our defense must be too. We need deeper integration with global threat intelligence feeds.

## Bottom Line
We are at a crossroads. We can either build a resilient digital Nepal or continue being a "soft target" for global threat actors. The choice is ours, but the window is closing.
