## The Campaign

In late 2023 and early 2024, a sophisticated phishing campaign targeted students at NCIT (Nepal College of Information Technology) and affiliated institutions. The attackers impersonated the college examination portal to harvest student credentials.

## Attack Vector

### 1. Email Lure
Students received emails appearing to be from `exam@ncit.edu.np` with subject lines like:
- "URGENT: Exam Results Published - Verify Now"
- "Your Exam Registration is Pending"

### 2. Fake Portal
The phishing link led to `nclt-edu.np` (note the 'l' instead of 'i')—a near-perfect clone of the legitimate portal.

### 3. Credential Harvesting
After victims entered their credentials, they were redirected to the real NCIT website, making the attack nearly invisible.

## Scale and Impact

Estimates suggest 500+ students may have entered credentials before the campaign was identified. Attackers used harvested credentials to:
- Access student email accounts
- Request password resets for connected services
- In some cases, access linked eSewa accounts

## Technical Indicators

| Indicator | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Phishing Domain | nclt-edu.np |
| Hosting | Bulletproof hosting (Malaysia) |
| SSL Certificate | Let's Encrypt (free, easy to obtain) |
| Email Sender | Spoofed header, actual origin was Gmail |

## Who Was Behind It?

The sophistication level suggests either:
- A local threat actor with inside knowledge of academic schedules
- An international group using Nepal-specific social engineering

## Lessons for Institutions

1. **SPF/DKIM/DMARC**: Email authentication would have flagged the spoofed sender.
2. **Domain Monitoring**: Institutions should monitor for typosquatting domains.
3. **Security Training**: Regular phishing simulations for students and staff.
4. **2FA Enforcement**: Mandatory two-factor for all academic portals.

## What Students Should Do

- Always check the URL before entering credentials
- Enable 2FA on all accounts
- Report suspicious emails to IT departments
- Never trust "URGENT" emails without verification
