## The Incident
In March 2025, Nepal's digital infrastructure suffered a coordinated assault. A large-scale Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack paralyzed over 400 government-linked websites. This widespread outage severely impacted critical services, notably disrupting the immigration portals at Tribhuvan International Airport (TIA), causing significant flight delays and passenger chaos.

Simultaneously, while IT teams were distracted by mitigating the volumetric DDoS attack, a secondary data breach occurred. Sensitive data originating from the Prime Minister’s Office (PMO) was exfiltrated and subsequently offered for sale on underground cybercrime forums.

## The Impact
The dual-pronged attack highlighted the fragility of Nepal's government IT infrastructure. The DDoS attack caused immediate operational paralysis at key national gateways, while the PMO data breach exposed sensitive state information, raising severe national security concerns.

## Lessons Learned
1. **DDoS as a Smokescreen**: Attackers frequently use loud, disruptive DDoS attacks to mask quieter, stealthy data exfiltration efforts. Security teams must monitor backend database access logs even during frontend outages.
2. **Robust DDoS Mitigation**: The government must implement advanced, cloud-based DDoS scrubbing centers that can absorb terabytes of junk traffic before it hits the National Information Technology Center (NITC).
3. **Zero Trust Architecture**: Government networks require strict zero-trust segmentation to prevent lateral movement. A compromise in one web-facing server should not grant access to sensitive PMO databases.
