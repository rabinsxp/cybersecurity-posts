## The Incident
In July 2025, the Ministry of Education's centralized portal was compromised by malicious actors. The attackers exploited vulnerabilities in the website's backend, gaining unauthorized access to massive databases containing the academic and personal records of Nepalese students and citizens.

The stolen data—which included full names, phone numbers, email addresses, citizenship numbers, and detailed academic transcripts—was subsequently leaked on Telegram channels and dark web forums.

## The Impact
The exposure of such comprehensive Personally Identifiable Information (PII) created an immediate and severe risk of identity theft for thousands of individuals. Scammers could use this data to forge documents, bypass identity verification on digital wallets, or launch highly targeted phishing campaigns against the victims.

## Lessons Learned
1. **Mandatory Vulnerability Assessments**: All government portals, especially those handling citizen PII, must undergo regular, rigorous Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT).
2. **Data Encryption**: Sensitive PII and academic records must be encrypted at rest. If the database is dumped, the data should be unreadable without the decryption keys.
3. **Incident Response and Reporting**: This incident highlighted the need for strict adherence to the newly enacted Nepal Cyber Security Law 2024, which mandates rapid incident reporting and transparent communication with affected citizens.
